# Email Sequence Types

Complete breakdown of each sequence type with structure, timing, and examples.

---

## Welcome Sequence

**Purpose:** Introduce yourself, set expectations, deliver immediate value, begin relationship.

**Trigger:** New subscriber

**Length:** 3-5 emails

**Timing:** 
- Email 1: Immediately
- Email 2: Day 1
- Email 3: Day 2-3
- Email 4: Day 4-5
- Email 5: Day 7

### Welcome Sequence Structure

**Email 1: Delivery + Introduction**
- Deliver what they signed up for
- Quick intro (who you are)
- Set expectations (what emails to expect)
- CTA: Consume the lead magnet

**Email 2: Quick Win**
- Provide immediate value
- Solve a small problem
- Build credibility
- CTA: Try this one thing

**Email 3: Your Story**
- Why you do this
- Your journey/credibility
- What you believe
- CTA: Reply or engage

**Email 4: Best Resource**
- Share your best content
- Give massive value
- No selling
- CTA: Check out this piece

**Email 5: What's Next**
- Recap the journey
- Introduce next steps
- Soft mention of paid offers
- CTA: Varies

### Welcome Email Templates

**Email 1 Structure:**
```
Subject: Here's your [lead magnet]

[Brief personal greeting]

[Delivery of promised item]

[Quick context on what it is/how to use]

[What to expect from these emails]

[Sign off]

P.S. [One quick tip or tease of next email]
```

## Nurture Sequence

**Purpose:** Build trust, deliver value, warm up for future offers.

**Trigger:** After welcome sequence (or ongoing)

**Length:** 5-10 emails (or ongoing)

**Timing:** Every 2-3 days

### Nurture Sequence Structure

No rigid structureâ€”focus on consistent value delivery.

**Content Types to Mix:**

1. **Teaching**: How to do something
2. **Stories**: Lessons from experience
3. **Proof**: Case studies/results
4. **POV**: Your opinions on industry topics
5. **Behind-the-scenes**: What you're working on
6. **Curated**: Best things you've found

### Nurture Email Pattern

```
[Hook/Opening thought]

[Main value/teaching/story]

[Lesson or takeaway]

[Soft CTA - reply, read more, try something]
```

**Key Principles:**
- 80% value, 20% (or less) promotion
- Build anticipation for emails
- Consistency matters more than perfection
- Personality throughout

## Sales Sequence

**Purpose:** Convert subscribers to customers for a specific offer.

**Trigger:** Manual (when you're selling) or automated (after nurture)

**Length:** 5-7 emails

**Timing:** Daily or every 2 days

### Sales Sequence Structure

**Email 1: Problem Agitation**
- Name the pain
- Make them feel understood
- Hint at solution
- No pitch yet

**Email 2: Solution Introduction**
- Introduce your offer
- Connect to the problem
- High-level benefits
- CTA: Learn more

**Email 3: Proof/Social Proof**
- Results and testimonials
- Case studies
- Specific numbers
- CTA: See how it works

**Email 4: Objection Handler**
- Address biggest objection
- Provide reassurance
- Guarantee mention
- CTA: Still have questions?

**Email 5: Urgency/Deadline**
- Why act now
- What they'll miss
- Final reminder
- CTA: Last chance

**Email 6: Final Call** (if needed)
- Deadline emphasis
- Summary of value
- Clear, simple CTA
- Accept those who won't buy

### Sales Email Pattern

```
[Hook - often story or question]

[Bridge to the offer]

[Value/benefit focus]

[Proof element]

[CTA - specific and clear]

P.S. [Urgency, bonus, or reassurance]
```

## Launch Sequence

**Purpose:** Maximize sales during time-limited campaign.

**Trigger:** Launch start date

**Length:** 6-10 emails over 5-14 days

**Timing:** Daily (or twice daily at key moments)

### Launch Phases

**Phase 1: Pre-Launch (before cart opens)**
- Build anticipation
- Seed the concept
- Address objections early
- Create waitlist/early interest

**Phase 2: Open Cart**
- Announce availability
- Full pitch
- Early bird incentives

**Phase 3: Mid-Launch**
- Proof stacking
- Objection handling
- Case studies
- FAQs

**Phase 4: Close Cart**
- Urgency intensifies
- Final objection crush
- Last chance messaging
- Cart close reminder

### Launch Sequence Structure

**Day 1: Cart Open**
- The big announcement
- Full offer details
- Early bird bonus
- CTA: Buy now

**Day 2: Why Now**
- Urgency reasoning
- What changes after launch
- Social proof
- CTA: Don't miss this

**Day 3: Proof Day**
- Testimonials
- Case studies
- Results
- CTA: Join them

**Day 4: Objection Crusher**
- FAQ format
- Main objections addressed
- Risk reversal
- CTA: Questions answered?

**Day 5: Story Day**
- Personal or customer story
- Transformation narrative
- Emotional connection
- CTA: Your turn

**Day 6: 48 Hours Left**
- Deadline reminder
- Recap value
- Scarcity real
- CTA: Time's running out

**Day 7: Final Day**
- Morning: Final day reminder
- Afternoon: Hours left
- Evening: Last chance
- CTA: Closes tonight

## Re-engagement Sequence

**Purpose:** Revive inactive subscribers before removing them.

**Trigger:** No opens/clicks in 30-90 days

**Length:** 3-5 emails

**Timing:** Day 0, Day 3, Day 7

### Re-engagement Structure

**Email 1: We Miss You**
- Acknowledge they've been quiet
- Ask if they still want emails
- Offer value to re-engage
- CTA: Click to stay

**Email 2: Best Of**
- Recap what they've missed
- Your best content
- Easy wins
- CTA: Check this out

**Email 3: Last Chance**
- Clear warning
- Will be removed if no action
- Easy to stay subscribed
- CTA: Click to stay

**Email 4: Goodbye (if no response)**
- Removed from list
- Door open to return
- No hard feelings

## Onboarding Sequence

**Purpose:** Activate new customers, reduce refunds, create success.

**Trigger:** Purchase

**Length:** 5-7 emails

**Timing:** 
- Email 1: Immediately
- Then every 1-2 days

### Onboarding Structure

**Email 1: Welcome + Access**
- Confirm purchase
- Access/login details
- What to do first
- Support info

**Email 2: Quick Win**
- First action to take
- Simplest path to value
- Celebrate small win

**Email 3: Core Feature/Module**
- Most important element
- How to use it
- Why it matters

**Email 4: Common Mistakes**
- What to avoid
- Save them frustration
- Show you care

**Email 5: Success Story**
- How others succeeded
- Inspire continued use
- Community/support

**Email 6: Check-In**
- How's it going?
- Ask for feedback
- Offer help

**Email 7: What's Next**
- Advanced features
- Additional resources
- Upsell (if appropriate)
