# The 9 Newsletter Formats

Complete guide to each format with structure, examples, and best practices.

---

## Format 1: Curated

**What It Is:** Collection of links, resources, or content from around the web with your commentary.

**Best For:**
- People who consume lots of content anyway
- Busy audiences who want filters
- Building authority through curation
- Lower time commitment

**Structure:**
```
[Brief personal intro - 1-2 sentences]

```

**Keys to Success:**
- Add opinion/commentary‚Äîdon't just aggregate
- Maintain consistent categories
- Quality over quantity (5-7 links max)
- Include why you chose each item

**Examples:** The Hustle, TLDR, Morning Brew

## Format 2: Educational

**What It Is:** Teaching a concept, skill, or framework in each edition.

**Best For:**
- Subject matter experts
- Building authority through teaching
- Audiences looking to learn
- Creating evergreen content

**Structure:**
```
[Hook - problem or curiosity]

[Concept introduction]

[Main teaching content]
- Point 1 with example
- Point 2 with example
- Point 3 with example

[Application/how to use this]

[Quick summary]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- One clear concept per edition
- Specific examples
- Actionable takeaways
- Progressive depth over time

**Examples:** James Clear, Lenny's Newsletter, Sahil Bloom

---

## Format 3: Story-Driven

**What It Is:** Narrative-first approach using stories to convey ideas.

**Best For:**
- Natural storytellers
- Building emotional connection
- Memorable content
- Personality-led brands

**Structure:**
```
[Story opening - situation/scene]

[Story development - conflict/tension]

[Story resolution or turn]

[Lesson/insight extraction]

[Application to reader]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- Start in the action
- Specific details that create vivid pictures
- Clear connection between story and takeaway
- Vulnerability when appropriate

**Examples:** Tim Ferriss (5-Bullet Friday), Khe Hy

---

## Format 4: Behind-the-Scenes

**What It Is:** Transparent look at your work, business, or journey.

**Best For:**
- Founders building in public
- Authenticity-first brands
- Audiences who want the real story
- Creating relatability

**Structure:**
```
[What happened this week/month]

[Numbers/metrics (optional but powerful)]

[What worked]

[What didn't work]

[What I'm thinking about next]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- Genuine transparency (not curated perfection)
- Include actual numbers when possible
- Share failures, not just wins
- Extract lessons others can apply

**Examples:** Baremetrics, indie hackers, many solopreneurs

---

## Format 5: Opinion/POV

**What It Is:** Your perspective on industry topics, trends, or events.

**Best For:**
- Strong viewpoints and expertise
- Thought leadership building
- Audiences who want analysis
- Differentiating from competitors

**Structure:**
```
[Topic/situation introduction]

[Conventional view or what others think]

[Your contrarian or nuanced take]

[Supporting reasoning]
- Evidence point 1
- Evidence point 2
- Evidence point 3

[Implications/what this means]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- Have actual opinions (not mealy-mouthed takes)
- Back up opinions with reasoning
- Acknowledge counterarguments
- Be willing to be wrong publicly

**Examples:** Stratechery, Not Boring, The Diff

---

## Format 7: Interview

**What It Is:** Q&A or conversation-based content with guests.

**Best For:**
- Access to interesting people
- Networking while creating content
- Varied perspectives
- Building relationships

**Structure:**
```
[Guest introduction - why they're worth reading]

[Context for the conversation]

Q: [Question 1]
A: [Answer - edited for clarity]

Q: [Question 2]
A: [Answer]

[Key takeaways]

[How to follow/learn more about guest]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- Better questions yield better content
- Edit for readability
- Include your commentary
- Balance guest value with audience value

**Examples:** The Profile, various Q&A formats

---

## Format 8: Challenge

**What It Is:** Structured series guiding readers through an activity or transformation.

**Best For:**
- Highly engaged audiences
- Skill-building topics
- Launch audiences
- Community building

**Structure:**
```
[Challenge context - where we are in the journey]

[Today's challenge/task]

[Why this matters]

[How to do it]
- Step 1
- Step 2
- Step 3

[Examples]

[Accountability CTA - share your results]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- Clear, achievable daily/weekly tasks
- Build toward meaningful outcome
- Include community element
- Celebrate participant wins

**Examples:** 30-day challenges, email courses, cohort-based learning

---

## Format 9: Hybrid

**What It Is:** Combination of multiple formats with consistent sections.

**Best For:**
- Varied content to share
- Audiences with diverse interests
- Flexibility without chaos
- Most established newsletters

**Structure:**
```
[Main piece - educational, story, or opinion]

---

üìö [Section 2 - curated links or resources]

---

üí° [Section 3 - quick hits, thoughts, or updates]

---

[Closing + CTA]
```

**Keys to Success:**
- Consistent sections each time
- One "anchor" piece of substance
- Clear visual separation
- Balance depth and breadth

**Examples:** Most successful newsletters evolve to hybrid

## Format Selection Guide

### By Available Time

| Time/Week | Best Formats |
|-----------|--------------|
| < 1 hour | Curated, Roundup, Behind-the-Scenes |
| 1-2 hours | Educational, Story, Hybrid |
| 2-4 hours | Opinion/POV, Interview |
| 4+ hours | Challenge (requires planning) |

### By Audience Need

| Audience Wants... | Best Formats |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Quick insights | Curated, Roundup |
| Deep learning | Educational, Interview |
| Connection with you | Story, Behind-the-Scenes |
| Strong opinions | Opinion/POV |
| Accountability | Challenge |
| Variety | Hybrid |

### By Your Strengths

| If You're Good At... | Try... |
|---------------------|--------|
| Consuming/filtering content | Curated |
| Teaching concepts | Educational |
| Telling stories | Story-Driven |
| Having opinions | Opinion/POV |
| Building relationships | Interview |
| Being transparent | Behind-the-Scenes |

## Switching Formats

If your current format isn't working:

1. **Announce the change** to subscribers
2. **Explain the new value** they'll get
3. **Test for 4-6 editions** before judging
4. **Ask for feedback** from engaged readers
5. **Iterate based on response**

Format isn't permanent‚Äîevolve as you learn what works.
