# Humanizing SEO Content

How to sound like a person, not a robot optimizing for robots.

---

## The Problem

Most SEO content reads like it was written by an algorithm for an algorithm.

Signs of robotic SEO content:
- No personality or perspective
- Every section same structure and length
- Generic examples (or no examples)
- Throat-clearing introductions
- No opinions
- Could have been written about any topic

This content might rank, but it won't:
- Build trust
- Generate shares
- Convert readers
- Create fans

---

## Human Writing Markers

What makes writing feel human:

### 1. Opinions and Perspective

Robots don't have opinions. You do.

**Generic (robotic):**
"There are many email marketing tools available, each with their own advantages."

**Human:**
"ConvertKit is my pick for creators. It's not the cheapest, but it's the most intuitive—and intuitive means you'll actually use it."

**How to add perspective:**
- State what you actually think
- Explain why you believe it
- Acknowledge counterarguments
- Take a stance

---

### 2. Specific Examples

Generic content says "for example..." then gives something obvious.
Human content tells specific stories.

**Generic:**
"For example, a business might use email marketing to nurture leads."

**Human:**
"Last month, a 5-email sequence brought in $12K for our course launch. The third email—the one addressing the 'I don't have time' objection—generated 40% of that revenue."

**How to add specificity:**
- Use real numbers from real experiences
- Name actual tools, people, situations
- Include details that couldn't be made up
- Share your own results/failures

---

### 3. First-Person Voice

Third-person creates distance. First-person creates connection.

**Distant:**
"Marketers should consider testing their subject lines."

**Connected:**
"I test subject lines obsessively. It's the highest-ROI activity I do—15 minutes of work that determines whether thousands of people open or ignore what I wrote."

**When to use first-person:**
- Sharing experience or opinion
- Giving advice based on what worked
- Creating connection with reader
- Making recommendations

---

### 4. Conversational Transitions

Robotic content moves section to section mechanically.
Human writing flows like conversation.

**Robotic:**
"Now that we've covered topic A, let's move on to topic B."

**Human:**
"But here's where most people get stuck..."
"The surprising part?"
"That said..."
"Which brings me to..."

---

### 5. Varied Structure

AI content often produces identical structures:
- Same paragraph lengths
- Same section lengths  
- Lists that all have 5 items
- Predictable rhythm

**Human writing varies:**
- Short punchy paragraphs when making points
- Longer paragraphs for nuance
- Lists only when lists make sense
- Different section depths

---

### 6. Acknowledgment of Nuance

Real expertise includes knowing when advice doesn't apply.

**Generic:**
"Follow these steps to succeed."

**Human:**
"This works for most businesses—but if you're in a highly regulated industry, you'll need to modify step 3. Here's why..."

**How to add nuance:**
- Acknowledge when advice doesn't apply
- Address potential objections
- Note exceptions
- Share what didn't work (not just what did)

---

## Kill These AI Tells

Phrases that scream "AI wrote this":

### Opening Clichés
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the digital age..."
- "When it comes to [topic]..."
- "Let's dive in..."
- "If you're like most [people]..."

### Filler Phrases
- "It's important to note that..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "That being said..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "The fact of the matter is..."

### Hedge Language
- "Can potentially help..."
- "May or may not..."
- "There are various ways..."
- "It depends on many factors..."

### Generic Enthusiasm
- "Exciting opportunities..."
- "Amazing results..."
- "Incredible value..."
- "Game-changing strategies..."

### Meaningless Transitions
- "Let's explore..."
- "It's time to discuss..."
- "Now let's take a look at..."
- "Moving on to the next topic..."

## The Humanization Process

### Before Writing

1. **Find your angle**: What's your specific take?
2. **Gather specific examples**: From your experience
3. **Identify opinions**: What do you actually think?
4. **Note nuances**: Where doesn't standard advice apply?

### While Writing

1. **Write in your voice first**, optimize second
2. **Speak sentences aloud** before typing
3. **Add specifics** as you go, not after
4. **Include your perspective** in every section

### After Writing

**Run through this checklist:**

- [ ] Would I say this out loud to someone?
- [ ] Are there specific examples from real experience?
- [ ] Do I state actual opinions?
- [ ] Have I removed AI clichés?
- [ ] Does sentence length vary?
- [ ] Is there personality in the transitions?
- [ ] Would a reader know this was written by me specifically?

---

## Brand Voice Integration

If a brand-voice profile exists:

1. Load the profile before writing
2. Apply voice patterns throughout
3. Check for voice consistency after drafting
4. Run through voice tests

Human + branded is the goal. Generic AI voice is the enemy.

---

## Balance with SEO Requirements

The goal isn't to ignore SEO—it's to satisfy both.

**Non-negotiable for SEO:**
- Keyword in title and first 100 words
- Logical header structure
- Comprehensive coverage
- Internal links

**Where humanity lives:**
- How you introduce the topic
- The examples you choose
- Your perspective and opinions
- Transitions between sections
- Voice throughout

Structure for Google. Voice for humans.
